Background

A decline in ethical behavior among junior high school students in Indonesia has become increasingly visible. Peer pressure, social media, and limited role models during ages 11–15, a critical stage of identity formation, are causing this change. Classroom with Superpowers acts as a response to that gap, turning ethics education into something students actually want to engage with.

Design Objective

To design an interactive medium that makes ethics feel relevant and fun, letting junior high school students experience the real weight of their choices through scenarios, consequences, and rewards.

Game Overview

Game Concept

Classroom with Superpowers simulates junior high school life through everyday interactions, where ethical actions become the key to winning. Illustrated scenarios that grow from simple to complex help players build long-term ethical habits through repetition and in-game consequences.

Goal

The goal of the game is to perform as many ethical actions as possible to earn in-game points called "appreciation stars", which represent how ethical behavior makes others appreciate you and see you as a good person.

Win Conditions

Earn 24 Appreciation Stars
Obtain the most appreciation stars in the end of the calendar

Research

Values

Play Board

Idea

The board game takes place in a school setting to help the target audience easily visualize and relate to it, with six different illustrated navigable areas, including the school hallway.

Moving Rules

Follow the navigational line to move based on the dice. Upon entering room starts with the black plot, and it is optional depending on their strategy to go there or not. Once they are in they have to follow the track of the room.

This is the detention room, where players can only enter if they commit serious unethical actions caused by the action wheel on the next pages.

School Calendar

Idea

Functioning as a game limitation, this school calendar tracks daily actions across 20 weekdays. At the end, players reflect on whether their student showed more ethical or unethical behavior throughout the month.

How Does It Work

Cards collected are placed on the calendar from day one to twenty and cannot be reused, creating strategic choices. The player with the most stars when the calendar is full wins.

Obtained cards are put in the calendar both ethical and unethical up to 20 days.

Action Wheel

Idea

The wheel represents moments where students invite others into an activity together, with outcomes that add or subtract appreciation stars, or move players to a new spot. Succeed or fail, everyone shares the result.

How Does It Work

Before spinning, the player chooses one to three others to join depending on where the wheel lands. Selected players cannot avoid the outcome, and the effect applies to everyone involved.

Careful

Landing on detention means skipping two turns for self reflection. Leave early by giving up two stars or rolling a six, otherwise wait it out before returning to the classroom.

Ethical Cards

Idea

Ethical cards present real life school scenarios across different rooms, giving players relatable examples through engaging visuals that reflect the four principles of ethics, integrity, empathy, responsibility, and respect in everyday school life.

How Does It Work

Landing on a green check circle allows the player to draw a white card from their current room, earning appreciation stars from (+1) to (+4). If no cards remain in that room, the player receives none.

Unethical Cards

Idea

Unethical cards represent negative behaviors every student should avoid, reflecting actions that break all four principles of ethics and carry real consequences, both socially and personally within the game.

How Does It Work

Landing on a red cross circle means committing an unethical action, requiring the player to draw a dark themed card from their current room. Consequences reduce appreciation stars from (-1) to (-4).

Superpower Cards

Active Type

Idea

Active superpower cards represent special abilities players can use to support their decisions and encourage more ethical actions at the right moment throughout the game.

How Does It Work

Active cards are used when needed based on each card's function. Two types exist, before cards used prior to rolling the dice, and after cards used following the roll.

Event Type

Idea

Event cards create environmental effects that influence players to move toward certain areas for a better chance at earning more stars.

How Does It Work

When activated, players within the indicated area earn an extra (+2) stars for every ethical action performed in that zone.

Event cards are placed in a designated spot, and any new card drawn replaces the previous one, activating the latest effect immediately.

Starting Setup

Place the cards according to their assigned rooms, except for superpowers cards and hallway cards, which have their own designated areas. At the beginning of the game, each player chooses a character and starts with five appreciation stars. The characters are placed on the start box right at the center.

Packaging Design

The packaging design is created to hold all board game components in an attractive and eye catching box that enhances visual appeal and draws attention. Beyond storage, the box serves as the first impression of the game, designed to feel exciting and inviting on a shelf while staying consistent with the brand identity inside.

Character

Each character embodies a principle of ethics, reflecting their personality and bringing the values closer to the players. Their iconic glowing hair and eyes symbolize the superpowers that have been bestowed upon them.

Brand Identity

Logo With Background Color

Logo Clearspace

Logo Concept

The logo brings together four character silhouettes converging toward a single point, representing unity and a shared ethical journey. Stars highlight achievement, irregular triangles add controlled chaos, and the overall design communicates learning through a playful and fun experience.

Printing

Minimum Size

Restrictions

Typography

The brand uses bold typography to express its energy and playful spirit. It mirrors the unpredictability of the game while remaining clear and engaging.

Colors

Bold and playful colors drive the brand identity, each representing a core ethical value while keeping the visuals lively and the principles of the game present in every element.
