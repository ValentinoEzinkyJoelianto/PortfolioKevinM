These illustrations are built on three core elements: concept story, visual language, and small details. Each piece begins with a narrative foundation, translated through thoughtful composition, color, and form, then refined with subtle details that enrich the world and invite deeper exploration to the audience.

Bon Voyage, Espers

Nobody gets left behind on a summer trip. Bon Voyage, Espers brings the Dislyte espers together at a sun-soaked dock, bags packed and vibes immaculate. This artwork is created for the Dislyte Summer Bash Contest 2025, the piece earned 2nd place, and yes, the duck made it too.

Not Every Love Story Blooms Freely

This flower shop in the middle of nowhere looks ordinary enough, until it does not. While the florist works quietly, a fly finds itself trapped in a terrarium, separated from a venus flytrap next door who might just be waiting for love or maybe food. Artwork created for the Plants and Flowers 44th International CSP Illustration Contest.

Facing Horde of Rimbala's Monster

The Rimbala is vast, dangerous, and full of things that should not exist. Rei, a brave boy, moves through it all, facing hordes of monsters and the gigantic carnivorous plant that surely will bring a lot of trouble. It is a wild adventure into the unknown, but do not worry, as his little blue furred bunny is always by his side. This artwork is created for the Andalas Fanart Contest, winning as Runner Up.

Blazing Sparks

Glowing lights, late night hangout, and metal tops spinning like the good old days. The two Canvasathor mascots, Vato and Cana, are back at it, celebrating a moment together the way they always have. Above the sparks, small versions of themselves appear, young and fully in the game, feeling the best moments all over again. This artwork is created for Light Up CANVASaTHOR: Five Years of Spark Contest.

Winter in Hokkaido

Somewhere in the far north of Japan, three friends and a very opinionated shiba inu settle into the best kind of winter night. Snowy hills, a glowing Christmas tree, a sky train drifting past, and a snow neko being built by them in which dog absolutely did not vote for this. Created for the J-Illust Contest 2024.

Yadnya Kasada

Every year, people from different backgrounds gather to climb Mount Bromo together, carrying offerings of crops and farm animals as a way of returning to the mountain what nature has given them. This moment reflects the diversity within the community, where generations, beliefs, and identities come together in a shared act of gratitude. More than a ritual, it is a living tradition that connects people to one another and to the land they depend on. This illustration is developed as a bottle design concept for Teh Botol's Kembali ke Tradisi Asli Indonesia 2026 celebration.
